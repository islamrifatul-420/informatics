My Cycling Tips & Little Experiences

I started cycling for fun, and slowly it became a part of my life. Here I share simple things I learned from my early rides in Japan.

Getting Started

Before you go for long rides, get used to handling the bike, braking smoothly, and keeping a steady speed. These small basics make your longer trips a lot easier.

Prepare for Your Ride

Bring water, check your tire pressure, and make sure your phone has enough battery. Japan’s cycling paths are safe, but it’s better when you’re prepared.

Route Ideas

If you’re new, try small city routes first. I started around Ikebukuro and slowly expanded my distance until I reached Mizuhodai — and later completed my first 46 km ride.
